Jay L. Cunningham, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor, Human-Computer Interaction

Lab's Guiding Research Question

How can we equitably design and evaluate human-centered AI/ML systems, particularly natural language technologies, to ensure they are fair, culturally competent, and reflective of the values and needs of all people—especially disproportionately affected users, while addressing the technical, ethical, and societal challenges inherent in AI/ML technologies?

Dr. Jay L. Cunningham is a technology scholar-practitioner with over a decade of interdisciplinary research, public interest technology leadership, and policy advisory experience at the intersection of AI governance, data ethics, and racial equity.

As the co-founder and CEO of AmendAI, Dr. Cunningham leads a public benefit corporation developing ethical data governance infrastructure and AI dataset certification tools specifically designed for generative AI and ML systems. At DePaul University, he directs the RAISE Lab, advancing participatory research on responsible AI and democratic data governance.

Dr. Cunningham serves as AI Policy and Research Fellow at the Washington State Attorney General's Office, contributing to state-level AI accountability frameworks, data governance policies, and technology risk assessments. His academic research, published in premier venues such as ACL, CHI, DIS, and TOCHI, critically examines the financial and societal impacts of algorithmic harms and inequities embedded in AI/ML systems.

He brings extensive experience convening cross-sector coalitions through his roles with the Social Science Research Council's Just Tech program, the University of Washington's HCDE Black Community Advisory Board, and partnerships with industry leaders at Google's People + AI, Apple Human-centered AI/ML, and Microsoft Research Fairness in AI.
