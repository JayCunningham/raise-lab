Research

Advancing responsible AI systems through community-centered research that addresses the technical, ethical, and societal challenges of artificial intelligence

Our Guiding Research Question

How can we equitably design and evaluate human-centered AI/ML systems, particularly natural language technologies, to ensure they are fair, culturally competent, and reflective of the values and needs of all people‚Äîespecially disproportionately affected users, while addressing the technical, ethical, and societal challenges inherent in AI/ML technologies?

Research Pillars

1

Socio-technical Harm Measurement

Developing community-informed frameworks for measuring AI harm that center affected communities' experiences and expertise, with particular focus on natural language technologies and culturally competent evaluation processes.

Natural Language Technologies Community Evaluation Cultural Competence Bias Detection

Current Projects:

  • Community-Informed LLM Evaluation Framework
  • Participatory Algorithmic Auditing Tools
  • ASR Performance Disparities for AAE Speakers
2

Responsible Data Economy

Creating ethical frameworks for data practices in AI ventures that ensure fairness and cultural competence, with community ownership and benefit from data used in AI systems, especially for disproportionately affected users.

Data Governance Community Ownership Financial Inclusion AI Ventures

Current Projects:

  • AmendAI Ethical Data Certification Platform
  • DataCommons Community-Centered Governance
  • Financial Materiality of Responsible AI Practices
3

Global South AI Equity

Addressing AI inequities affecting Global South communities through hyperlocal language technologies and international research partnerships that preserve cultural heritage while advancing technological capability.

Language Preservation International Collaboration Cultural Alignment Policy Influence

Current Projects:

  • Ghanaian Language Speech Recognition (with UG)
  • Hyperlocal LLM Cultural Sensitivity Auditing
  • Global Community Advisory Board Network
4

Human-Centered AI & Technology Design

Investigating design methodologies, interaction paradigms, and implementation strategies for intelligent systems that are meaningfully integrated into human experiences and environmental contexts, prioritizing user agency and contextual appropriateness.

Interaction Design User Experience Design Methods Contextual Computing

Current Projects:

  • Participatory AI Interface Design
  • Community-Centered Design Toolkits
  • Contextual AI System Implementation

Featured Research Projects

Active

Community Advisory Board Model Implementation

Developing and documenting the Community Advisory Board methodology for participatory computing research, with case studies from UW HCDE Black CAB and international applications.

Key Collaborators:

UW HCDE Black Community Advisory Board, Dr. Julie Kientz, Dr. Daniela Rosner

Active

Equitable Speech Recognition for Ghanaian Languages

International collaboration developing speech recognition systems for Akan, Ga, Twi, and Ikposo languages, with 5,000+ hours of collected speech data and community-centered evaluation approaches.

Key Collaborators:

Prof Isaac Wiafe (University of Ghana), Mark Atta Mensah, UG HCI Lab Team

Active

AmendAI: Ethical Data Governance Platform

Building infrastructure for ethical data governance and AI dataset certification, specifically designed for generative AI and ML systems with community consent frameworks.

Key Collaborators:

AmendAI Team, Community Data Partners, Industry Stakeholders

Planning

Chicago Civic Technology Initiative

Developing partnerships with Chicago community organizations and city government to create responsible AI solutions for local challenges while building workforce development pathways.

Key Partners:

DePaul CDM, Chicago Community Organizations, City of Chicago (planned)
