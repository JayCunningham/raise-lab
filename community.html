Our Community-Centered Approach

At RAISE Lab, we believe that the most impactful and ethical AI systems emerge from authentic partnerships with the communities they're designed to serve. We don't just study communities—we learn from them, partner with them, and ensure they lead in defining what responsible AI means.

Our Community Advisory Board (CAB) model exemplifies this philosophy, creating structured pathways for community voices to shape research questions, methodologies, and outcomes. Every partnership is guided by principles of mutual benefit, cultural responsiveness, and community ownership.

Community partners aren't research subjects—they're co-researchers, co-designers, and co-leaders in our mission to advance computing for the common good.

Community Advisory Board (CAB) Model

Transforming University-Community Research

The Community Advisory Board model represents a fundamental shift in how universities engage with marginalized communities in technology research. Rather than extractive consultation, the CAB creates genuine power-sharing partnerships where community members have decision-making authority over research directions and outcomes.

Developed through our work with the UW HCDE Black Community Advisory Board, this methodology ensures that research serves community needs while maintaining academic rigor. The model has been successfully implemented and is being adopted by other institutions seeking authentic community engagement.

Learn About Implementation

Core CAB Principles

  • Community Leadership: Community members co-lead research design and implementation
  • Decision-Making Power: Real authority over research questions and methodologies
  • Mutual Benefit: Clear value and outcomes for community partners
  • Cultural Responsiveness: Research that respects and reflects community values
  • Sustainable Relationships: Long-term partnerships beyond individual projects
  • Capacity Building: Skills transfer and empowerment for community members
  • Transparency: Open communication about research processes and outcomes
  • Accountability: Community-defined success metrics and evaluation

Chicago Civic-Tech Engagement

The RAISE Lab, College of Computing and Digital Media, and DePaul University are committed to strong civic engagement in our research, innovations, teaching, and impact. We are on a mission to re-define the tech landscape for Chicago locally toward impact on a national and global scale.

🏛️Community Action & Advocacy

We're excited for opportunities to support local community action, research, and advocacy on issues pertaining to technology. Our lab serves as a bridge between academic research and grassroots organizing for technology justice.

💼Workforce Development

We hope to contribute to Chicago's emerging technology workforce, looking for opportunities to create pathways for community members to compete for jobs and launch innovative ventures in the responsible AI space.

⚖️AI Policy and Regulatory Governance

The RAISE lab is well equipped to contribute to the rapidly-growing technology policy and governance landscape. We provide research, policy guidance, and expert analysis to help elected officials responsibly shape AI policy. We actively collaborate with academic researchers, community residents, legal professionals, and business leaders on AI policy expertise.

🌆Local Innovation Ecosystem

By centering Chicago's diverse communities in our research, we aim to create technology solutions that reflect the city's values while contributing to national and global conversations about responsible AI.

🤝Civic Partnerships

We actively seek partnerships with Chicago civic organizations, city government, and community groups to ensure our research serves local needs while building national models for community-centered technology.
