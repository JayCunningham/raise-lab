About RAISE Lab

Advancing responsible AI systems through human-centered research that serves the common good of humanity and the environment

Our Mission

The DePaul RAISE Lab (Responsible AI Systems and Societal Experiences) advances research, public knowledge, and innovation on the social and ethical responsibilities of computing and human-centered technologies. We design, study, critique, and strategize policy of sociotechnical systems to promote equity, accountability, cultural alignment, and justice in computing‚Äîparticularly for communities underserved by technology.

Through participatory research methods, interdisciplinary collaboration, and commitment to public interest innovation, we aim to ensure that computing systems serve the common good of humanity and the environment. Our work bridges academic rigor with community partnership, creating technology that empowers rather than marginalizes.

Our Guiding Research Question

How can we equitably design and evaluate human-centered AI/ML systems, particularly natural language technologies, to ensure they are fair, culturally competent, and reflective of the values and needs of all people‚Äîespecially disproportionately affected users, while addressing the technical, ethical, and societal challenges inherent in AI/ML technologies?

Founded in 2025 under the leadership of Dr. Jay L. Cunningham, RAISE Lab represents a new model for responsible AI research‚Äîone that genuinely centers community voices while maintaining the highest standards of academic excellence. We believe that the most impactful and ethical AI systems emerge from authentic partnerships with the communities they're designed to serve.

Lab Culture & Values

RAISE Lab cultivates an environment where academic excellence meets community accountability. We believe that the best research emerges from diverse perspectives, authentic collaboration, and a shared commitment to justice.

Our lab community includes students from undergraduate through doctoral levels, industry affiliates, community partners, and international collaborators. We prioritize mentorship, interdisciplinary learning, and creating pathways for underrepresented students to lead in responsible AI research.

Every lab member contributes to our mission of computing for the common good, whether through technical research, community engagement, policy analysis, or innovative application development.

Core Values

  • Community-Centered Excellence: Research quality measured by community benefit
  • Participatory Innovation: Co-design with affected communities, not for them
  • Global Justice: Local engagement with international perspective
  • Interdisciplinary Collaboration: Breaking down silos for holistic solutions
  • Environmental Responsibility: Technology that serves planet and people
  • Academic Integrity: Rigorous methods with transparent processes
  • Student Empowerment: Developing the next generation of responsible technologists
  • Public Interest: Research that serves the common good

Our Vision for the Future

RAISE Lab envisions a future where AI systems truly serve the common good‚Äîwhere technology empowers communities, preserves cultural heritage, and advances justice rather than perpetuating harm. We're building toward a world where community voices lead AI development, where cultural competence is standard practice, and where the benefits of artificial intelligence reach everyone.

Governance frameworks that prioritize community benefit and justice in AI development and deployment.
